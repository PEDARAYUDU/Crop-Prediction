Problem Statement

Farmers frequently face challenges in selecting the most suitable crops for their fields, often depending on advice from peers and government guidelines. However, these decisions typically overlook essential factors such as soil characteristics, pH levels, weather conditions, and market prices, leading to lower yields and profitability. As a result, there's a need for a more data-driven approach that ensures optimal crop selection for both economic gain and environmental health.

Project overview

We are developing a Django-based web application that integrates a machine learning model to predict or suggest the most suitable crop for a farmer's field. This application leverages data on soil characteristics, weather conditions, and market prices to provide tailored crop recommendations, aiming to optimize both profitability and sustainability.

Data Description

The dataset consists of 4,400 rows and 9 features, including values for nitrogen, potassium, phosphorus, temperature, humidity, pH, rainfall, price, and crop name. The dataset is equally distributed across different crops, with 200 rows allocated to each crop.

Performance and Evaluation

The bar plot illustrates the accuracy of different machine learning models used for crop classification. The Random Forest model achieved the highest accuracy at 98.75%, closely followed by XGBoost at 98.41% and SVM at 98.18%. The Decision Tree model also performed well, with an accuracy of 97.05%. Logistic Regression and Naive Bayes models had lower accuracies, at 91.82% and 89.66%, respectively. This suggests that ensemble methods like Random Forest and XGBoost are particularly effective for this classification task.

The Random Forest model demonstrated outstanding performance in classifying crops based on the given features, achieving 100% accuracy on the test set as indicated by this confusion matrix. This suggests that the model is highly reliable and can be trusted for crop prediction tasks in this context.

Conclusion

The Crop Suggestion System (CSS) project successfully leverages advanced machine learning techniques, including Random Forest, SVM, XGBoost, and others, to accurately predict the most suitable crops for farmers based on key features like soil nutrients, weather conditions, and market prices. With a dataset equally distributed among various crops, the Random Forest model achieved near-perfect accuracy, as demonstrated by the confusion matrix, indicating the model's robustness in making precise predictions without misclassification. This project not only empowers farmers with data-driven crop selection but also enhances agricultural productivity and sustainability by integrating environmental factors and economic considerations into decision-making.
